SOCIOLOGY MAJOR REQUIREMENTS (UIUC)
====================================
Total Hours: 32 hours of Sociology credit
Minimum GPA: 2.0 in major and overall

REQUIRED COURSES:
-----------------
1. Introductory Sociology Course (3-4 hrs)
   SOC 100: Introduction to Sociology
   Description: An overview of the topics of sociology and of various sociological 
   perspectives, and of how scholars in the discipline study social phenomena.
   Note: This course is a prerequisite for most two-hundred level courses.
   Credit: 4 hrs
   *If using transfer credit for Introductory Sociology, some schools grant only 3 credits for SOC 100.*

2. Introductory Statistics Course (3-4 hrs)
   SOC 280: Introduction to Social Statistics
   Description: An introduction to the statistical measures and techniques used in the 
   social sciences. First course in social statistics for students without mathematics 
   beyond the high school level; topics include the role of statistics in social science 
   inquiry, measures of central tendency and dispersion, simple correlation techniques, 
   contingency analysis, and introduction to statistical inference; includes the statistical 
   analysis of social science data using personal computers.
   Prerequisite: SOC 380
   Credit: 4 hrs
   
   *Other courses that meet this requirement (but do NOT count toward sociology hours):*
   - STAT 100
   - PSYCH 235
   - ECON 202
   - MATH 161
   - Parkland College: MATH 108 and MATH 160

3. Introduction to Sociological Theory (3 hrs)
   SOC 200: Introduction to Sociological Theory
   Description: This course is an introduction to the foundations of sociological theory. 
   Topics may include the problem of social order and the nature of social conflict; 
   capitalism and bureaucracy; the relationship between social structure and politics; 
   and the evolution of modern societies.
   Prerequisite: Sophomore standing
   Credit: 3 hrs

4. Social Science Research Methods (4 hrs)
   SOC 380: Social Science Research Methods
   Description: Introduction to the foundations of social research and to the major types 
   of research methods employed in sociology. Provides exposure to the major tools and 
   terminology of social research, including the use of computers in sociology. Topics 
   include: research design, finding and using sociology literature, measurement, sampling, 
   survey research, field methods, use of available data, quantitative data analysis and 
   presentation, and computer resources for research.
   Credit: 4 hrs

5. Capstone Experience (3 hrs) - Choose ONE from the list below:

   A. SOC 400: Internships
      Description: Selected internship opportunities in which student and faculty member 
      develop a program of study and research related to internship.
      Credit: 0 to 3 undergraduate hours
      Grading: Approved for Letter and S/U grading
      Repeatable: May be repeated to a maximum of 6 hours
      Prerequisite: Junior or senior standing; and SOC 100 or SOC 101 or SOC 163; and six 
      additional hours in Sociology or acceptance of faculty member and Director of 
      Undergraduate Studies.
      Application: Submit to Undergraduate Academic Advisor at soc-advising@illinois.edu
      Note: Requires INTERNSHIP LEARNING CONTRACT
      Credit: 3 hrs

   B. SOC 450: Senior Capstone Seminar
      Description: Over the course of the semester, students will conceive and execute an 
      original sociological research project, using their knowledge of the sociological 
      literature developed in substantive courses and their skills in data collection and 
      analysis developed in methods courses. In parallel, students will explore professional 
      opportunities in sociology and engage in professional development activities, including 
      exploring opportunities for graduate education and learning skills in job search, and 
      resume, c.v., personal statement and cover letter development.
      Credit: 3 hrs

   C. SOC 495: Senior Honors Seminar
      Description: Intensive scrutiny of current literature on one selected topic. Critical 
      reading and discussion followed by writing essays and research proposals. Subject will 
      shift yearly. There may be community work as an aspect of this course.
      Credit: 3 undergraduate hours
      Repeatable: May be repeated to a maximum of 6 hours
      Prerequisite: For sociology majors only. Student must have at least 3.5 grade-point 
      average in sociology courses and consent of instructor.
      Note: This course is 1 of 2 options for graduating with departmental distinction.
      
      Example: Junior Dale Robbennolt (Civil Engineering + Sociology) completed a personal 
      research project in SOC 495: "Faculty Perceptions of Gender in Engineering Education 
      and Group Work" - presented at UIUC Undergraduate Research Symposium Spring 2021.

ADVANCED LEVEL COURSES:
-----------------------
- 12 hours of advanced hours in Sociology
- Select 300- or 400-level Sociology courses
- Advanced coursework from required courses listed above counts toward this total

ADDITIONAL SOCIOLOGY COURSES:
-----------------------------
- Students must earn a total of 32 hours of credit in sociology
- For transfer credit to count, it must be accepted by LAS as Sociology coursework
- Courses in other departments will NOT count as sociology hours unless they are cross-listed
- Example: STAT 100 fulfills statistics requirement but does NOT earn sociology hours
- Example: PSYCH 201 (Social Psychology) is NOT cross-listed, so no sociology hours

SUPPORTING COURSEWORK:
----------------------
Purpose: 12 hours of courses logically grouped around a theme outside Sociology

Restrictions (CANNOT use):
- Sociology department courses
- Courses cross-listed in Sociology
- Applied art courses (e.g., musical instrument courses)
- Courses that satisfy the foreign language requirement

Approval: Must see a Sociology advisor early in junior year for formal approval

Five ways to plan supporting coursework:
1. Take 12 hours of courses in ONE department
   Popular departments: Anthropology, Psychology, Political Science, Community Health, 
   Business Administration, Communication, Human Development and Family Studies

2. Assemble a cohesive package of 12 hours across departments
   Example: Pre-law students might take law-related courses in Political Science, 
   Business Administration, and History

3. Use courses taken towards a MINOR
   Available minors: Anthropology, Cinema Studies, Classics, Comparative Literature, 
   Computer Science, English, English as a Second Language, French, German, Greek, 
   History, Italian, Latin, Mathematics, Russian & East European Studies, Russian 
   Language and Literature, Spanish, Italian and Portuguese

4. Use courses taken towards an INTERDISCIPLINARY MINOR
   Examples:
   - African Studies
   - Afro-American Studies
   - Latin American Studies
   - Gender and Women's Studies

5. Use courses taken towards a DOUBLE MAJOR
   Common double majors: Psychology, Political Science
